Views Overview

Views provide a way for business teams to view, interact and manipulate critical data that can be key to the decision making process. Views are built and organized in the views module inside the Demand Management user interface. View collections and view collection sets may be organized within this module and are an integral part of the events, assortment planning, and promotions modules as well several others.

Through proper leverage of the Views module, Demand Management is highly extensible and customizable in the hands of a fully equipped technical resource. The Views module, in conjunction with the creation of SQL views, will provide the required toolkit for building custom reports and maintenance screens to accommodate any necessary custom workflows.

View hierarchy

It is important to understand the hierarchy associated with views to properly organize and display them. Groups may be created to help organize view collection sets, but contain no hierarchical data.

View Description
View Collection Set The highest level of view classification. View collection sets contain view collections, views, and widgets.
View Collection The next level of view classification. View collections are the highest level at which user roles may be assigned and the icon may be changed (listed in the edit screen as “image”).
Views The next level of view classification. A view is a collection of widgets used to display useful data in an easy to understand way.
Widgets Widgets are the lowest level of view classification in that a widget will be a part of a particular view. Widgets are a way for Demand Management to display data for the user. There are many varieties of widgets, including data grids, pivot grids, single column pivot grids, charts, forms, schedule, and browser.
