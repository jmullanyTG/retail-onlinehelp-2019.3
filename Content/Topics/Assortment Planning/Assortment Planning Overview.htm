Assortment Planning Overview

The Demand Management Assortment Planning module provides users with an innovative perpetual approach to the assortment planning process. Leverage scenario modeling capabilities through the Target Creation process to quickly lay a sound foundation for your assortments or forge an assortment from the ground up using manual placeholder creation. Once your plan framework is created, fine tune at multiple levels, and apply supplemental analyses from integrated supporting modules to craft your ideal buy strategy. Reconcile against financial targets and other goals through a variety of reporting tools, manage order commitments through integration with Order Manager and external systems, and ultimately, track your plan accuracy and make adjustments in-season.

The parameters you select during plan creation are the cornerstones of success in the assortment planning process. Business process discussions should be had prior to plan creation to understand the existing infrastructure into which plans will be integrated. If Merchandise Financial Planning and/or Allocations systems or processes are in place, assortment plans should be structured to facilitate the movement of data between them. Consider the following:

  • How is our business structured in terms of product? How many major categories are there and what subdivisions exist in each? What level of detail do we need inherent in our product hierarchy?
  • How is our business structured in terms of selling channels? Do you need to segment by full and off-priced business, by country, by geography, some combination of all? Are there other factors related to site attributes (capacity, climate, demographic, etc.) that we use to group sites together?
  • What is the structure of our financial reporting periods structured and what calendar is used to track them?
  • What will be our primary planning currency for financial targets and comparisons? Are there additional taxes or costs of doing business internationally to consider?
  • What are the primary metrics and/or attributes against which our business will be measured?

Points to consider

It is vital that sufficient time is given to discuss the points presented above and to the pre-requisite steps outlined thereafter. Additionally, by taking the time to consider and set plan defaults that are appropriate to your business, you will streamline your workflow and save yourself a great deal of time as you plan. Consider the following points:

  • Attributes: one of the strengths of the Demand Management Assortment Planning solution is that users have the ability to define business segments that are not directly tied to your product hierarchy. Spending time to strategize among your team and define what attributes are meaningful and then structuring your plan accordingly is a great way to uncover and address areas of opportunity with your plans
  • Cluster Sets: cluster sets are the cornerstone of Assortment Planning in Demand Management. Carefully consider the areas of your business that you want to plan different assortments for and/or those that will be managed by different individuals and create your cluster sets accordingly
  • Price Translations: for companies doing business internationally, there may be additional costs associated that can decrease your profit margin – import/export fees, tariffs, higher real estate prices, etc. Demand Management offers price translations as a way of simply creating premium pricing by product hierarchy level, price range, and locale to help recoup those extra costs, if this is part of your corporate pricing strategy
  • Plan Defaults: Demand Management Assortment Planning offers a wide array of defaults covering everything from cluster sets, seasonality, and size curves, to constraints such as periods of supply and pack tolerance % that are utilized as part of receipt planning and prepack optimization calculations. These can always be overridden later if needed, but taking the time to set defaults to cover the majority of cases will save you a great deal of time later
