Best practices in Promotions planning

Promotions planning is designed to give the user a great deal of flexibility when modeling promotional effects for products within plans. However, there are best practices that can maintain a high level of efficiency and help users get the most out of this area of Assortment Planning.

Using defined promotions as templates

As promotions do not have ties to specific products or levels of the site hierarchy, they can be used over and over for other areas of your business to which you want to apply the same basic promotion.

In the above example, the same base promotion has been applied to three distinct product areas, including different cluster sets, start and end dates, and even with different Final Indices and Hold Sales settings.

The same AUR Effect and Behavior is applied to all applications, but aside from that, as long as applications occur within the Eligibility Start and End Dates (or the Is Always Eligible option is checked), the user retains a great deal of flexibility with the specifics of how the promotion is applied to each area.

This same methodology is mirrored to an extent within the details of each application as well. Start and End dates, Final Indices, Hold Sales options, and even participation in the promotion application can be varied at the placeholder/cluster level. Making use of the ability to make changes at the application and detail levels will help minimize the number of promotions users need to create and maintain.

Using the Is Always Eligible option

If you have one or more standard promotions you use often, using the Is Always Eligible option ensures they are always available for application.

Suppose you find yourself running 20% off promotions frequently as you plan. In the above example, you simply create one promo with a 20% off Promotion Discount % and the Is Always Eligible option checked and you will be able to apply that promo with different Final Indices to different products and site hierarchy levels without ever have to specify or extend Eligibility Start or End Dates or create duplicate promotions to achieve the same effect.

Holding sales versus not holding sales

The Hold Sales option allows users to choose whether a given promotion application can change the overall magnitude of a product’s sales plan or simply shift the magnitude of sales in each period while still adhering to the original sales plan.

An example of the Hold Sales option unchecked

Prior to the activation of the promotion application, a product has a Ttl Sls (U) of 33,881 over its life and a ROS U of 200.48 per site per period:

After activation of the promotion application, which has a Final Index of 1.5 on each cluster, the Ttl Sales (U) has increased to 41,963 and ROS U to 248.30. This is because the Hold Sales option is not checked for either cluster the product is assorted to allowing the overall sales plan to increase.

An example of the Hold Sales option checked

The following screenshot shows the sales units by period for a product prior to the promotion application being activated. The Promotion Index for each period included in the promotion (wk06 – wk12) is set to 1.00, indicating no change.

After the promotion application has been activated, but because Hold Sales is checked for both clusters, Ttl Sales (U) and ROS U remain the same as before.

However, after activation the Promotion Index for the promotional periods is 1.50 and the sales per period has increased during the promotional periods and decreased in the non-promotional periods, compared to before the promotion application was activated.
