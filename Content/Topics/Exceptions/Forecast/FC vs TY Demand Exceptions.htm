FC vs TY Demand Variance Exceptions

The FC vs TY Demand Variance exception flags level forecasts when the forecast (FC) for the next 3 periods is exceedingly above or below the item's sales history for the prior 3 periods of this year (TY).

Formula

This exception is based on the variance in demand (calculated as a percentage) between the upcoming and last year periods. The formula for determining the percentage of demand variance is: 

(Forecast - Demand History) / Demand History

Where:

  • Forecast
    = the sum of Effective Forecast quantities for the upcoming number of periods (default is 3 periods).
  • Demand History
    = the sum of Total Sales quantities for the prior number of periods (default is 3 periods).

A positive value resulting from this calculation occurs when the forecast for the upcoming periods is greater than the demand for the prior periods. A negative value occurs when the forecast for the upcoming periods is less than the demand for the prior periods

Whether the resulting value constitutes an exception depends on the variance threshold defined in the exception filters.

  • The default number of periods examined for this exception is 3 and includes the current period.
  • If either the 
    Forecast
    or 
    Demand History
    value is equal to zero, the variance percentage will equal 100%.

Filter conditions

To filter for forecasts with FC vs TY Demand over-forecast exceptions

(From the Level Forecast Exceptions field group:

    • Field LevelForecastFCvSalesPercent is more or equal to 3 (or whatever positive percentage value you want to be the threshold).
    • Field LevelForecastReviewed is False.

To filter for forecasts with FC vs TY Demand under-forecast exceptions

From the Level Forecast Exceptions field group:

    • Field LevelForecastFCvSalesPercent is smaller or equal to -0.75 (or whatever negative percentage value you want to be the threshold).
    • Field LevelForecastReviewed is False
