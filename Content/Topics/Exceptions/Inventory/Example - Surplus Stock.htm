Example - Surplus Stock

Surplus stock exceptions identify situations when incoming orders are projected to cause an item to be overstocked at a location beyond a specified number of days. In such situations, it may be best to cancel or defer the surplus orders.

You can use filters and views to create an ad-hoc Surplus Stock report in Inventory Manager that displays your surplus stock exceptions. In this example, we'll use a pre-created exception filter that identifies items with at least one upcoming surplus order and 60 days of projected excess stock.

Instructions

  1. From the Manage tab, click Inventory Manager.

  2. In the Items dialog box, select the Inventory Exception: Surplus filter.

  3. In the Choose Fields dialog box, select the Inventory Exception: Overstock (Surplus) view.

  4. In the item document, you can see all of the items that exceed the Excess Stock Days threshold specified in the filter.

    For this example, the threshold is "more or equal to 60 days".

  5. In the grid, you can sort items by priority ("A" items listed first) and review each item's Excess Stock Days, Excess Stock Quantity, Excess Stock Value, and more.

  6. In the Details pane, review an item's Stock Holding Projection tab to see how the incoming orders affect the projected amount of excess stock at the location over time.

  7. Click on the Inbound and Outbound Orders tab in the Details pane to see the upcoming surplus order details.

  8. Review other tabs in the Details pane as desired to get a better understanding of the forecasted demand, inventory policy, and other information governing the item stock at the location.

Potential Actions

Actions you can take to resolve surplus stock exceptions include:

  • Canceling or deferring surplus orders until stock quantities are balanced with demand.
