Managing Inventory Exceptions

Demand Management can report items which are projected to be overstocked or understocked at any of your locations. By reviewing these items, you can determine what course of action to take.

Overstock exceptions

Overstock exceptions indicate items which Demand Management projects will have excess and/or surplus stock.

  • Excess stock - There is projected to be more stock on hand than is required to meet demand. Consider redistributing stock to other sites.
  • Surplus stock - There is projected to be more stock arriving from upcoming orders than is required to meet demand. Consider canceling or deferring orders.

Understock exceptions

Understock exceptions indicate items which Demand Management projects will have shortfalls and/or potential stock outs before a normal replenishment order can arrive.

  • Shortfall - Items for which safety stock must be sold to meet projected demand. Consider creating and/or expediting orders for these items, or redistributing stock from other sites.
  • Potential stock out - Items which are projected to be completely out of stock before a normal replenishment order can arrive. Consider creating and/or expediting orders for these items, or redistributing stock from other sites.

Resolving inventory exceptions

Resolving an inventory exception may involve one or more of the following actions:

  • Canceling or deferring surplus orders.
  • Expediting open orders.
  • Creating an order manually.
  • Adjusting the item's demand forecast.
